# SECTION 2: Complete Strategy Documentation

## Strategy #1: The "One & Done" — Elder's Core Supply & Demand + Order Flow System

This is Elder's primary strategy, extracted from multiple video transcripts. This is the system he uses daily.

### Overview
- **Name:** One & Done (OAD)
- **Philosophy:** Take ONE high-quality setup per day, execute it perfectly, walk away
- **Type:** Multi-timeframe trend-following supply & demand with order flow confirmation
- **Win Rate:** Not explicitly stated, but designed for high R:R (risk-to-reward)

### Timeframes (Triple Screen Approach)

| Timeframe | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **4-Hour** | Trend direction / Market structure |
| **30-Minute** | Supply & Demand zone identification |
| **5-Minute** | Trade execution & entry timing |

### Step-by-Step Entry Rules

#### Step 1: 4-Hour Trend Analysis (Direction Filter)
- Check the 4-hour chart EVERY MORNING before trading
- **Bullish:** Higher highs and higher lows → ONLY take longs
- **Bearish:** Lower highs and lower lows → ONLY take shorts
- **Range-bound:** Equal highs and equal lows → Play within the range only (buy demand, sell supply within range)

#### Step 2: 30-Minute Supply & Demand Zone Identification
- On the 30-minute chart, identify supply and demand zones
- **How to draw zones:** Look for **consolidation / neutral candle(s) BEFORE an explosive move**
  - For DEMAND: Find the consolidation before a big explosive move UP → draw a box around those consolidation candles
  - For SUPPLY: Find the consolidation before a big explosive move DOWN → draw a box around those consolidation candles
- Use the box/rectangle drawing tool on your chart
- These zones represent where institutional orders are resting

#### Step 3: 5-Minute Execution (Entry)
- Wait for price to approach your identified supply or demand zone on the 5-minute chart
- **For LONGS (off demand, 4H bullish):**
  1. Wait for price to enter the demand zone on 5-min
  2. Look for a **higher high** on the 5-min (structure shift bullish)
  3. Enter on the **higher low** after that higher high
  4. Confirm with order flow (see Step 4)

- **For SHORTS (off supply, 4H bearish):**
  1. Wait for price to enter the supply zone on 5-min
  2. Look for a **lower low** on the 5-min (structure shift bearish)
  3. Enter on the **lower high** after that lower low
  4. Confirm with order flow (see Step 4)

#### Step 4: Order Flow Confirmation (Bookmap)
This step "greatly increases your win rate" — it's the edge that separates Elder's system from basic S&D trading.

**For SHORT entries (in supply zone):**
1. Open Bookmap or any Level 2 / Time & Sales visualization tool
2. Watch for **absorption**: Buying pressure gets absorbed by passive sellers (buyers are buying aggressively but price stops going up — sellers are absorbing all the buy orders)
3. OR watch for **exhaustion**: Buying pressure dies out — the green bubbles (buy orders) get smaller and smaller
4. **Entry trigger:** Wait for a SELLER to fill a passive buy offer and create a LOWER PRICE (price ticks down)
5. Once that happens → enter short immediately

**For LONG entries (in demand zone):**
1. Watch for selling pressure to get absorbed by passive buyers OR exhaustion of sellers
2. **Entry trigger:** Wait for a BUYER to fill a passive sell offer and create a HIGHER PRICE
3. Once that happens → enter long immediately

**Alternative entry (from live trade video):**
- Wait for a **liquidity sweep** into the zone (price spikes into the zone grabbing stop orders)
- See heavy stop orders showing up on order flow
- Enter on the sweep with stop below the sweep low

### Exit Rules

#### Stop Loss
- **For LONGS:** Stop loss placed BELOW the swing low that tested the demand zone (below the sweep if there was a liquidity sweep)
- **For SHORTS:** Stop loss placed ABOVE the swing high that tested the supply zone
- The stop is tight — just below/above the zone reaction point

#### Take Profit Targets (Priority Order)
1. **VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)** — Primary target
2. **Resting pre-market liquidity** — Previous session highs/lows, overnight highs/lows
3. **Any resting liquidity visible on Bookmap** — Large passive orders sitting in the book

### Risk Management

- **Pre-trade question:** "If I lose this trade, will I be mad at myself?" — If yes, DON'T take the trade
- This usually means: position size is too large OR the setup isn't part of the plan
- **Small losses are tuition** — they're the cost of doing business
- **Winners must be big enough** to cover losses and still move forward
- **Protect downside, let upside take care of itself**
- **NEVER revenge trade** — getting mad leads to doubling size, revenge trading, tilt
- **NEVER catch a falling knife** — wait for the move to finish, THEN enter

### Position Sizing
- Not explicitly stated in videos, but implied:
  - Risk should be small enough that a loss doesn't cause emotional reaction
  - Account swings of ~2% mentioned as significant ($181K on what appears to be ~$9M account)
  - Suggests risking 1-2% of account per trade maximum

### Best Market Conditions
- **Trending markets** (4H making clear HH/HL or LL/LH)
- Works in crashes too — hedge long-term portfolio with puts while day trading the short side
- Avoid trading during Trump tweets / unexpected fundamental events (wait for the move to finish)
- Works on ANY instrument with supply/demand dynamics

## Strategy #2: Crash/Bear Market Hedging Strategy

Extracted from the market crash videos:

### Rules
1. During market crashes, **BUY shares** of major companies (SPY, TSLA, AAPL, AMZN, MSFT, NVDA) at discount prices
2. **Hedge with SPY puts** — buy 2 months out (for rollover optionality)
3. If market structure shifts bearish on higher timeframe → **stop buying puts**
4. All profits from puts → reinvest into buying more shares at cheaper prices
5. Keep doing this until the market structure shifts bullish on higher timeframe
6. Then stop buying puts → all discounted shares run up

### Key Rule
- **Don't try to guess the bottom** — wait for the market to TELL you via structure shift
- Don't buy SPY calls thinking "we hit the bottom" — you don't know

## How to Read the Signals

### Supply Zone (Short Setup)
1. 4H is bearish (lower highs, lower lows)
2. On 30M, you see a box drawn around consolidation before a big drop — that's your supply zone
3. Price rallies back up into that zone on 5M
4. On 5M, you see a lower low form (structure shifts bearish)
5. On Bookmap: green bubbles (buying) get smaller OR get absorbed (price stops rising despite buying)
6. A red print (sell) fills a passive bid → price ticks down = ENTER SHORT
7. Stop above the swing high that tested supply
8. Target: VWAP or nearest liquidity pool below

### Demand Zone (Long Setup)
1. 4H is bullish (higher highs, higher lows)
2. On 30M, box drawn around consolidation before a big rally — that's your demand zone
3. Price drops back into that zone on 5M
4. On 5M, you see a higher high form (structure shifts bullish)
5. On Bookmap: red bubbles (selling) get smaller OR get absorbed
6. A green print (buy) fills a passive offer → price ticks up = ENTER LONG
7. Stop below the swing low that tested demand
8. Target: VWAP or nearest liquidity pool above

# SECTION 6: Key Lessons from Elder Santis

## Trading Psychology (The Real Edge)

From transcript analysis, these are Elder's core psychological principles:

1. **"My strategy isn't the issue. My issue is myself."** — After tracking every trade for 9 months, he found that removing all off-plan trades would have made him 6 figures. The strategy worked; his discipline didn't.

2. **"If I lose this trade, will I be mad at myself?"** — The #1 pre-trade filter. It catches overleveraging, boredom trades, and FOMO trades.

3. **"Small losses are tuition"** — Accept them as cost of business. The danger isn't the loss — it's your REACTION to the loss (revenge trading, doubling down).

4. **"Don't catch the falling knife"** — Wait for the move to finish, THEN enter. Applies to crashes, news events, Trump tweets.

5. **"A 5-year-old can do this strategy"** — The analysis is simple. The hard part is mastering emotions, discipline, and consistency.

6. **"Every successful trader went through the same thing"** — Blown accounts and years of losses are NORMAL. Most people quit because they think something is wrong with them.

7. **"Think like a professional, not a gambler"** — Focus on process, not outcomes. Protect downside, let upside handle itself.

## The Data-Driven Aha Moment

Elder's turning point: He tracked every trade for 9 months. He was down $50K. He then removed every trade that was:
- Overleveraged
- A revenge trade
- Not following the plan

Result: He would have made **six figures** that year. The strategy was profitable — HE was the problem. This shifted his focus from finding better strategies to improving himself.
